Proposal
The Disaster Management Department functions under the aegis of the State Disaster Management Authority (SDMA) and works as per the guidelines of the National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA).
The department is responsible for planning, coordinating, and implementing various activities aimed at mitigating the impact of natural disasters and weather-induced hazards such as cyclones, floods, cyclones, coastal erosion, landslides, and extreme weather events. Through these efforts, the department plays an important role in strengthening disaster-resilience across the state by implementing measures such as disaster risk mitigation, preparedness, response, relief, and rehabilitation.
For planning, response and decision-making processes at the department level for effective disaster management.
It is vital to have timely, accurate, and reliable information available. However, since the nature of disaster events has to be dynamic and time-sensitive, it is often challenging to collect and aggregate relevant information during emergencies. As a result, due to the limited availability of historical data sets, analytical specifications, and evidence-based decision-support mechanisms, many aid and response measures are implemented only in the form of reactive responses when the situation arises. This calls for the creation of a systematic and centralized data repository containing disaster-related historical information to support an informed and proactive decision-making process.
